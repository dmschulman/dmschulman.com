Good website design should morph and evolve over time to meet the changing needs of the user, but it can be extremely challenging to introduce new design paradigms into an existing design system.

With this in mind, I was tasked with both designing and developing a series of refreshed web components which could be placed on existing page templates to upgrade the look and feel of key landing pages.

This work started with our [Financial Aid](https://www.sju.edu/financial-aid) landing pages and was later incorporated 
 into our [Location](https://www.sju.edu/location) page, [Neighborhoods](https://www.sju.edu/neighborhoods) section, and continues to grow across various top-level destination pages as we revisit and improve our content and web experience.

Many of the designs were understood as an evolution of existing elements in our component library. Our goal was not to reinvent the site's design but to update the look as well as to build a modern collection of individual parts we could bolster across all web components moving forward. This would align our future development efforts as well as allow us to replace more dated looking components and sunset design that no longer fit the university's brand.
