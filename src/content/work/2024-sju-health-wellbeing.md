Though our team primarily focuses on marketing-related efforts, we reliably partner with every area of the SJU campus community. In 2024 the Division of Student Life hoped to engage with us to create a website that could help promote a new curriculum they had developed to help students thrive in every aspect of their lives.

Working alongside a designer in our department, web and creative helped to scaffold an interactive landing page based on the requirements set forth by Student Life. Not only did they want an eye-catching visual to draw user's attention to the nine dimensions of health and well-being but they also hoped to highlight student stories as well as link to other resources across the SJU website that tie into these areas of personal development.

Playful design elements help shape the tone of the package and content within. This is not a clinical presentation, it's meant to spark curiousity and start a conversation. CSS shapes and nth-child rules were employed to create the different "blobby blotches" of color seen throughout the section.

Our marquee feature of this landing page is of course the icon wheel representing each dimension of health and well-being. On desktop users can click into each icon to reveal a summary and a link to learn more, an effect achieved purely through HTML and CSS using radio buttons and :checked pseudo-classes. And on mobile, we drop the interaction in favor of a simple list of cards that provide the same functional jumping off point.
