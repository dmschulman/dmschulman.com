Like many people who were planning to get married in 2020, the journey to our actual wedding day was a bit of a do-si-do.

Despite sending 3 separate RSVPs to our friends and relatives we ended up having "the big one" on July 9th, 2022. To get everyone excited for the occasion and to coordinate all the relevant details I put a simple website together over the course of those heady days in 2020 while fully remote. Though I initially intended to use JotForm for simple online RSVPs we ultimately decided paper postcards would be our best bet.

The site itself is compact, easy to read, and uses only named web-safe colors. Emojis were also a fun choice and, if you can believe it, still not widely adopted as a flourish on personal websites yet. The only heavy lifting on the page was a countdown (now a "count up") timer that dutifully ticked down the days until the wedding. Countdown.js was used here, specifically [moment-countdown](https://github.com/icambron/moment-countdown) due to its simplicity.
