Likely one of the most ambitious packages in recent Marketing & Communications history, we knew Saint Joseph's inaugural 30 Under 30 series would be a standout from the moment it was pitched.

Saint Joe's alumni have so many success stories to share and although we feature many of them across the website our communications team hope to try a new approach which included 30 profile stories, 5 videos, and an ambitious website and social media campaign. A bright energetic art style and top-notch video content, supplied by the creative team, brought the entire digital package together.

Participants were selected from 5 industry categories representing a diverse array of career backgrounds. To reflect this on the landing page we clustered each grouping of profiles under big bold category headings, anchoring them down the page when they clicked a button for a specific industry. I had some fun coming up with hover animations and transitions for these buttons.

Underneath each category are a series of "flip cards" featuring names and photos of each individual, and when interacted with, flip over to reveal more information and a link to their specific profile. Some categories had an uneven amount of cards so I stood up another card type which could contain a quote or other text content. The baseball card-like design language gave everyone a sense of excitement about this package and the drive to get it live.

Social media was a large part of the marketing strategy behind 30 Under 30 and it was necessary to make it for users to share out stories. In my profile page layout I included a handy-dandy social sharing bar the scrolls smoothly alongside the article content. A "back to top" button was also added to allay any fears about how long our wonderful page content ended up being.

But out of all these unique features, my favorite ended up being the hero image at the top of each article. Our creative team dreamed up each category's treatment and shot every profile picture, but due to concerns about image fidelity, assets weighed in around the 1mb mark, not to mention text embedded in the image simply wouldn't be accessible.

My solution involved a new component that would take all these individual pieces and fuse them together through the magic of PNG transparency and good ole CSS. The hero image component uses a background color value, a transparent PNG to layer in the zig-zags or curly-cue lines, and the profile photo which sat on top of it all. Positioning the important details about the individual as text on the page gave us everything we needed, and clocked in at around 200kb-400kb.

Overall, 30 Under 30 was extremely well received, garnering 30k visit in the first 30 days and over 500k impressions across our social media platforms. The effort also directly tied to admissions campaign materials and collateral and made a definitive mark on our 2028 and 2029 class recruiting efforts.
