After the successful digital transition of our bi-annual university magazine we soon decided to also adapt our annual university report. The university report features storytelling about current Saint Joseph's University students, faculty, and alumni, but also needs to tell the story of the university's financial position and strategic direction.

This [inaugural issue](https://www.sju.edu/news/university-report/2023-2024/) included over 20 news articles and feature stories that required extensive component development to present according to design specifications. A 3-part headline series on the [15th anniversary of the Kinney Center](https://www.sju.edu/news/university-report/2023-2024/15-years-kinney) included a variety of new experiences, such as a multimedia timeline component, embedded audio player, and slideshow featurette along with the usual text and image-heavy storytelling.

Another feature, this time on a news story about the [student-run community garden program at Saint Joseph's University](https://www.sju.edu/news/university-report/2023-2024/living-the-mission/sharing-saint-josephs-roots-vegetables-with-community), required an interactive design where users could click around an image to reveal nuggets of information about the garden. I used a radial animation to hint the clickable regions and a pure CSS solution to show/hide the information. At mobile sizes the information appears under the graphic and areas of the image are highlighted with numbers instead of interactive icons.

But our most [complex story](https://www.sju.edu/news/university-report/2023-2024/soar-campaign-hits-200-million), one that was requested towards the end of our development process, became the standout feature, resulting in a great deal of visibility for the SJU SOAR capital campaign and even encouraging tens of thousands of dollars in additional gifts. I was both the primary designer as well as the developer of this package.

While we only had a handful of facts and figures, we knew we needed to make the content pop. A variety of animated components, again leveraging only CSS and HTML, were drafted to tell the story of how much this campaign had raised and how the gifts had impacted our students and the university as a whole. New Photoswipe galleries were built to show off renderings of capital projects the donations would or had already begun financed.

Finally, a static presentation just wouldn't do. Our stakeholders needed "more". I devised a straightforward way to wrap all of our individual components and apply unique slide animations to really do something brand new across our magazine stories. The result was incredibly well received by internal and external audiences and has become a staple template for future university reports.
